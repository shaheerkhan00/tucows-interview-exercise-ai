DOMAIN SUSPENSION POLICY

Section 1: Suspension Reasons
Domains may be suspended for the following reasons:
1. Missing or invalid WHOIS information
2. Non-payment of renewal fees
3. Abuse complaints (spam, phishing, malware)
4. Legal orders or disputes
5. Violation of acceptable use policy

Section 2: WHOIS Requirements
All domain registrants must maintain accurate WHOIS contact information including:
- Full legal name
- Valid email address
- Current mailing address
- Working phone number

Section 3: Reactivation Process
To reactivate a suspended domain:
1. Update your WHOIS information with accurate details
2. Resolve any outstanding billing issues
3. Contact support with your domain name
4. Wait 24-48 hours for verification
5. Domain will be reactivated once verified

Important: You must update WHOIS within 15 days of suspension notice.

Section 4: Abuse-Related Suspensions
These suspensions also happen without any prior notice.
Domains suspended for abuse require:
- Escalation to abuse team
- Investigation (3-5 business days)
- Proof of remediation
- May require additional verification
